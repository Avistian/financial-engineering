# Pace correction — deeper explanations, slower ramp (learner feedback, 2026-07-23)

**Trigger.** After the Q1 checkpoint (Lesson 010), the learner said quant material *feels hard at the
current pace* and asked for **even better explanations, explicitly accepting longer lessons** as the
cost. This is a pedagogy/pace correction, **not** a mission change — the goal (Quant Researcher) and
curriculum are unchanged; only *how* lessons are taught changes.

**Calibration (asked, not assumed).** The learner selected the levers that would help most:
- **Intuition before symbols** — build the mental picture before any formula.
- **Smaller steps** — one new idea at a time; never two hard concepts stacked.
- **Re-warm the prerequisite inline** — don't assume undergrad math/prob (or an earlier lesson) is fresh.
- **A slow lane for derivations** — every algebra step shown and justified.

Not top-selected (still fine to use, just lower priority): more small examples, more visuals, plain-
English recaps.

**Where it bites.** The learner named **Lesson 008 (linear algebra/PCA)** and **Lesson 009
(regression)** as the hardest — i.e. the **math-heavy** units, not the conceptual/markets ones (001–005
felt fine). Read-through: markets vocabulary and stats-in-words land well; dense linear algebra and
estimator derivations are where the pace outran comprehension.

**Implication for what's next (important).** **Q2 (Units 011–020)** is the densest math on the entire
track — measure-theoretic probability, Brownian motion, the Itô integral, Itô's lemma, SDEs (GBM, OU),
and pricing a European option three ways. This is precisely the material most at risk under the old
pace. Treat Q2 as the primary test of this decision: slower ramp, a geometric/probabilistic picture for
every abstraction, one definition/operator at a time, prerequisites re-warmed inline, and a fully
worked slow-lane derivation (with each step justified) for the load-bearing results — Itô's lemma, the
Black–Scholes PDE, the OU solution. Expect these lessons to run well past the size band; that is fine.

**Guardrail.** Slow the *teaching*, not the *testing*. Retrieval (warm-ups, predict-before-reveal,
teach-backs, quizzes) stays as effortful as ever — desirable difficulty in practice is what builds
durable memory. We lower the cost of the first encounter, not the cost of recall.
