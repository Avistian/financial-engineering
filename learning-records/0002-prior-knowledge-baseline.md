# Prior-knowledge baseline (sets the entry point)

Stated at intake (2026-07-06):
- **Math:** solid undergrad (calculus, linear algebra, basic probability). **No measure theory / SDEs.** → Year 1 Q2 is a genuine bridge, not review; don't assume filtrations/Itô are known.
- **Programming:** strong — Python + C++/Rust, data structures, systems. → Labs can move fast on code; the difficulty budget goes to the *finance/stats*, and the C++/Rust systems units (Y3 Q3) can be paced aggressively.
- **Finance/markets:** little to none. → Year 1 Q1 builds vocabulary from zero; never assume prior market knowledge in lessons or quizzes.

Implication: front-load markets + stochastic-calculus foundations; keep labs Python-first; treat
interview coding (LeetCode) as maintenance, not new learning.
